Photoengraving and the Materiality of Twentieth-Century Comic Books

Ruth-Ellen St. Onge | Rare Book School at the University of Virginia
Posted 1 May 2020

Ruth-Ellen St. Onge is the Associate Curator & Special Collections Librarian at Rare Book School at the University of Virginia. She holds a doctorate in French and Book History and a Master of Information Studies degree from the University of Toronto. She is currently the Interim Editor of the journal The Papers of the Bibliographical Society of Canada. In 2018, Ruth-Ellen co-curated “Comics in Circuit,” a Rare Book School exhibition on the publishing history and materiality of comic books and graphic novels. She has also published scholarly articles and book chapters on the work of Ray Fawkes, Alan Moore, and Garth Ennis.

Recommended citation:

St. Onge, Ruth-Ellen. “Photoengraving and the Materiality of Twentieth-Century Comic Books.” Sequentials, vol. 1, no. 4, 2020.
